Recommendation — Based on Your Non-Negotiables

Occidental Tamarindo wins on nightlife — the only one of the three where you can walk out the front gate and be in a real town with bars, live music, and people. That's a hard advantage given it's one of your filters. The tradeoffs are real though: food is the weakest of the three, excursions cost extra, and housekeeping has been flagged in recent reviews.

Planet Hollywood wins on housekeeping, food variety, and excursion access — but it's isolated on the Papagayo Peninsula with no town to explore. If the group is happy staying on-property in the evenings, the Star Class butler tier and 8-restaurant spread make it the most seamless experience. The on-site casino and club are there if you want them.

Azura is the most intimate — adults-only with a boutique feel and solid food and activities — but Sámara's nightlife is the quietest of the three areas, and it's the furthest from the airport.

If nightlife is non-negotiable: Occidental Tamarindo. If quality and seamlessness are the priority: Planet Hollywood. If intimate and adults-only matters most: Azura.

Excursion Wishlist — Built Out

Every excursion on this list is achievable from all three properties. The Arenal Volcano and a proper coffee tour require an overnight or long day trip (4–5 hrs from Guanacaste, or a short domestic flight). Everything else — waterfall, kayaking, ATV — is within 30–90 minutes. A rental 4x4 unlocks the region on your own terms.
🌊
Water & Kayaking
Bioluminescent Night Kayak
Paquera Bay · Evening · ~$65/person

Paddle after dark while bioluminescent algae light up the water around your kayak — one of the few year-round spots on earth for this phenomenon. Tours run 5:45–7:30 pm. Group size capped at 10, so you could book the full boat privately.

Year-Round ~$65/person 2 hrs Evening
Tortuga Island Snorkel Cruise
Nicoya Gulf · Full day · ~$85–110/person

Boat cruise to Isla Tortuga — snorkeling over a volcanic reef with angelfish, moray eels, and spotted eagle rays. White sand beach time and fresh fruit included. One of the most popular full-day excursions from the Nicoya Peninsula and Tamarindo.

Top Rated ~$85–110/person Full day
Lake Arenal Kayak
La Fortuna · Combine with Volcano Day

Costa Rica's largest lake at the foot of Arenal Volcano — kayaking or stand-up paddleboarding with the volcano as your backdrop. Best combined with the Arenal day trip. Sunset boat cruises also available for a more relaxed version.

Combine w/ Volcano ~$60/person Half day
Private Sportfishing Charter
Pacific Coast · 4–8 hrs · ~$600–900 boat

Private inshore charter targeting yellowfin tuna, mahi-mahi, roosterfish, and wahoo. Experienced crews know the Pacific waters. Look for dolphins and whales on the way out — common in August.

Private Charter ~$600–900 whole boat Morning

Waterfalls & Jungle
Montezuma Waterfalls
Montezuma Village · 45 min · Free entry

Three cascading falls with natural swimming pools — the most famous waterfall on the Nicoya Peninsula. A 1 km jungle hike from the road. Swimmers can climb to upper pools for cliff jumping from 25m. The road trip itself is half the experience: river crossings, ocean views, and deserted beaches en route.

Bucket List Free (~$1.50 upper pools) Half day Bring water shoes
La Fortuna Waterfall
La Fortuna · Day trip or overnight

A 70m cascade into a crystal-clear pool at the base of Arenal Volcano. 500 steps down (and back up). Best combined with the Arenal day trip. Combo tours include the volcano hike, hanging bridges, and hot springs all in one day.

Combine w/ Volcano ~$20 entry Full day
Canopy Zip-Line
Cabo Blanco Reserve / Rincón de la Vieja · ~$55–75/person

Nearly a mile of zip-line through jungle canopy bordering Costa Rica's first protected reserve. 11 platforms high in the trees with ocean views and regular howler monkey sightings. Planet Hollywood guests can access the Rincón de la Vieja adventure centre, which includes zip-line, horseback, and hot springs as a combo.

~$55–75/person 2–3 hrs All levels
Wildlife Refuge Guided Tour
Curú / Cabo Blanco · Private · ~$80/person

Private guided tour with a certified naturalist through secluded beach trails. Spot howler monkeys, scarlet macaws, iguanas, and coatis. Fully customizable. Pairs well with the bioluminescent night kayak for a full nature day.

Private Tour ~$80/person Half day
☕
Coffee Tour
Don Juan Coffee & Chocolate Tour
La Fortuna · Best combined with Arenal

Hands-on farm experience covering the full bean-to-cup journey — plus cacao and sugar cane. The Arenal highlands produce some of Costa Rica's finest coffee. Guided tasting of multiple roasts included. Best positioned as part of the Arenal overnight so you're not making the trip twice.

Best Combo w/ Volcano ~$35–50/person 2–3 hrs
Café Monteverde Cloud Forest Tour
Monteverde · 5 hrs from Guanacaste

Generations-old coffee farm in the misty cloud forest — volcanic soil, high altitude, and family traditions over a century old. One of the most atmospheric coffee experiences in Costa Rica. Best as a Monteverde stopover rather than a standalone day trip from the coast.

Best: Monteverde stopover ~$30/person 1.5 hrs tour

Volcano
Arenal Volcano — Full Day or Overnight ✦ Signature Trip
La Fortuna · ~4–5 hrs drive or short domestic flight from Guanacaste

Costa Rica's most iconic volcano. Guided rainforest walks along the lava base, hanging bridges through the cloud canopy, La Fortuna Waterfall, Don Juan coffee tour, and the Tabacon Hot Springs (20+ thermal pools fed by the volcano, lit up at night) — all doable in one big day or a comfortable overnight.

Getting there: From Guanacaste (all three properties) it's roughly 3.5–4 hrs by car. The faster option is a short domestic flight from Liberia (LIR) or Tambor to La Fortuna — roughly 40 minutes. Highly recommended if doing this as a day trip without losing two full days to the road.

Signature Experience Domestic flight: ~$150–200pp each way Drive: 3.5–4 hrs from Guanacaste Best as overnight Hot springs included in most tours
Arenal Volcano Base Hike
Arenal National Park · Guided · ~$50/person

Guided rainforest hike along old lava flows with a naturalist. Mostly flat to moderate. You can't summit legally, but the base hike with wildlife spotting is the right experience. Go in the morning before clouds roll in.

~$50/person 3–4 hrs Morning
Tabacon Hot Springs
La Fortuna · Volcano-fed thermal pools

Costa Rica's largest network of naturally flowing thermal mineral springs — 20+ pools from 22–40°C at the base of Arenal. Available as a day or evening visitor pass even without staying at the resort. Most tour packages bundle it with dinner. Genuinely unforgettable at night.

Don't Skip This ~$70–90/person day pass Evening best

Car Rental

Get a 4x4 — especially in August.

August is green (rainy) season. Roads in and around the Nicoya Peninsula are unpaved, potholed, and can flood after heavy afternoon rains. A standard sedan will limit where you can go. Every operator recommends a 4WD — the added cost is negligible vs. the freedom it provides for day trips to Montezuma, Cabo Blanco, and beyond.

Best Vehicle

4x4 SUV — Hyundai Creta, Suzuki Jimny, or equivalent. High clearance, automatic preferred for group comfort. Fits 4–5 plus luggage comfortably.

Where to Pick Up

Pick up at Liberia (LIR) airport — closest to all three properties. Adobe Rent a Car and Jumbo Car both operate there. Adobe has the largest fleet in Costa Rica.

Cost in August

Green season = lowest rates of the year. ~$400–600 USD/week for a 4WD SUV. Book early — 4x4s are in higher demand than sedans and go fast.

Adobe Rent a Car — Largest Fleet in CR Jumbo Car — No Hidden Fees Budget / Avis at LIR Airport ~$400–600 USD/week for 4WD SUV in August

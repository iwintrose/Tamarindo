After Dark — Tamarindo Nightlife

OCHO Beach Club
Fancy open-air bar perched by the mangrove estuary. Electronic music, surprise sunset parties. Long tables for groups and intimate alcoves. A crocodile occasionally spotted from the deck. Perfect opener or closer to any evening.
Sunset / Electronic / Chic · Every night
Chiquita's
International DJs Thursday–Sunday. Tamarindo's premier techno and house venue. Elaborate cocktail menu, trendy restaurant side too. Opens 9 PM, peaks around 10:30. The undisputed go-to for electronic music.
Techno / House / International DJs · Thu–Sun
Pacifico Bar
Beloved by locals. Thursday reggae night is legendary — live bands, island vibe, rasta decor. Wednesday ladies night. Open till 2:30 AM daily. One of the most authentically Costa Rican nights out in Tamarindo.
Reggae / Reggaeton / Live Bands · Wed–Sun
Kinky Tamarindo 🏳️‍🌈
Vibrant, fully inclusive bar. Drag performances, creative cocktails (named Madonna, Rihanna, Cher, Amy Winehouse), outdoor dance floor with festive light effects. One of the most joyful, welcoming spots in town.
Drag / Inclusive / Festive · Every night
Pangas Beach Club
The most beautiful dinner setting in Tamarindo. Tables in the sand at the river mouth. Live music, wahoo carpaccio, lobster, sizzling volcanic beef. Reserve for Night 1 and Night 8 — both are milestone meals. Reservation required.
Fine Dining / Live Music · Reserve: reservations@pangasbeach.com
El Garito
Tamarindo's most underground club. Hidden in a passage inside a building — look for the sign. Open-ceiling dance floor, electro and house music all night. The destination for those who want to dance till sunrise.
Electro / House / Underground · Late night

Travel Notes

August Weather
Green season — mornings are clear and stunning. Rain rolls in mid-afternoon, usually warm and brief (30–60 min). Plan outdoor excursions to start before noon. Landscapes are lush, vivid, and far less crowded than peak season.
Getting Here
Fly into Liberia (LIR) — not San José (SJO). It's a 90-minute private shuttle to Tamarindo. Pre-book your group shuttle 2+ weeks out. Tamarindo Airport (TMU) exists for domestic connections if needed.
Money & Payments
USD and colones both widely accepted. Most restaurants and tours take credit cards. Carry some cash for local sodas, small vendors, and tips. ATMs are plentiful in town — BAC San José preferred. Bring USD from home.
What to Pack
Reef-safe sunscreen (mandatory — coral protection), rash guards for surf/snorkel, one nice outfit per night out, flip flops + closed-toe shoes for excursions, light layer for evenings, small dry bag for boat days.
Playa Grande Tip
Just across the estuary — a $1 boat ride, 5 minutes. Quieter, bigger waves, no crowds. August is leatherback sea turtle nesting season. Worth a half-day on any slow morning. The boat leaves whenever you walk up.
The Local Spirit
"Pura Vida" — pure life. It's hello, goodbye, you're welcome, no problem, life is good. It's the entire vibe of Tamarindo distilled into two words. Use it freely. Costa Ricans will love you for it.
